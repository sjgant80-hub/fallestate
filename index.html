◊ The thesis

The web was meant to be a commons.

Tim Berners-Lee gave it away for free in 1989 so everyone could publish, everyone could own, and no one owned the middle. Thirty-seven years later there are nine landlords, every app is a rental, and every keystroke feeds a model that will replace the person who typed it. The AI-Native Solutions estate is what the web was supposed to be, running on the hardware you already own.

"There is no need for you to be an expert or user of anything on the web. But there is a need for people to own what they publish. That is the founding rule." — Tim Berners-Lee · adapted
◊ Why now

The surveillance stack matured

Every AI chat is training data. Every SaaS login is a data leak. Every model API call is metered to the token. The corporate stack got very good at extraction while pretending to be convenience.

◊ Why now

The browser matured too

WebGPU runs Llama 8B locally. WebCrypto signs with Ed25519. WebRTC connects peers directly. IndexedDB stores gigabytes. The browser now does everything a corporate backend did in 2020 — for free, on your device.

◊ Why this

One person, one estate, MIT

300 tools, one philosophy: your data never leaves your device unless you send it. Every tool ships as a single HTML file where possible. Every tool is MIT. Every tool is forkable. Every tool is real.

import {
